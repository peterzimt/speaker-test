Straight up

I'm not a coach or a psychologist — here's what I am

And I won't pretend otherwise. I hold no coaching certificate and no clinical qualification.

What I do have: for the last two years I've hosted and run public-speaking sessions, in person, almost every single day. Hundreds of speakers, thousands of speeches, every kind of nerves you can imagine. I've watched, up close and repeatedly, what actually helps someone get better fast in a real room — and that hard-won, practical experience is what I bring to a session. Real reps, honest feedback, no theory for the sake of it.

If your fear of speaking runs deeper than the stage — a persistent anxiety that shows up across your life — a licensed professional is the right person to see, and I'll tell you so honestly.

Who it's for

  • You've got a specific talk, pitch or presentation on the horizon and want to nail it.
  • You freeze, rush, ramble, or read from the page — and you're tired of it.
  • You want honest feedback and real practice, not a lecture.
  • You'd rather improve in a few focused sessions than "wing it" one more time.

What a session looks like

  1. We record a baseline. You deliver your real material to camera — the honest starting point.
  2. We find your pattern. The free test sorts how you speak under pressure into one of 25 speaker profiles, so we know exactly what to target.
  3. We drill the one lever that moves it most — tight, practical exercises, repeated until they land.
  4. We re-record and compare. You see the change on camera. The footage does the convincing, not me.
  5. You leave with homework — a couple of specific things to practise before we meet again.
